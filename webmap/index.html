About this survey

Lufft MARWIS road-weather sensor + phone GPS, driven through north-east Belgrade during a rain event on . Times shown are local (UTC).

Sensor saturation caveats

  • Water film height clips at 6000 µm. The MARWIS optical sensor cannot report more than ~6 mm of water; over deep puddles it typically reads only about 1 mm because it only sees the top film. Values at 6000 µm are a lower bound, not a measurement.
  • Friction is bounded to 0.55–0.82. Readings at the 0.55 floor are censored: real friction may be lower (e.g. in standing water).
  • Points affected by either limit are flagged saturated and drawn with a purple ring on the map.
  • Points without GPS coverage (or with low-confidence forward-filled positions) are flagged gap and are not drawn (or drawn with reduced confidence).
